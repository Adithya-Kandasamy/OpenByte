Our Mission

Many services today are digital — government forms, banking, job applications. People who can't navigate technology are increasingly locked out of modern society.

OpenByte steps in to close that gap through free tech help sessions, accessible workshops, and community-first device support — tailored for elderly individuals, low-income families, and small local businesses.

Free and accessible to all
Community-driven and volunteer-led
Focused on real, practical skills
Partnered with local schools & libraries
What We Do

Our 3 Core Programs

Designed to meet people where they are — whether they've never touched a smartphone or just need a little help getting started.

Tech Help Sessions

One-on-one or small group help for anyone struggling with everyday technology. No judgment, no jargon — just friendly support.

Smartphones Emails Online Forms Banking Apps Maps

Workshops & Classes

Beginner-friendly group sessions held monthly at local schools, libraries, and community centers. Scalable and great for visibility.

Smartphone Basics Internet Safety Google Docs Scam Prevention

Device Setup & Support

Hands-on help setting up new devices, fixing common issues, and installing the apps that matter most in daily life.

New Devices Troubleshooting App Setup Data Transfer
Why It Matters

Real Impact. Real People.

Technology access isn't a luxury — it's a necessity. Here's why OpenByte's work changes lives.

Real-World Access

Government forms, banking, and job applications are now digital. We help people participate fully in modern society.

Independence

Especially for older adults, being able to navigate technology means freedom — not relying on others for everyday tasks.

Economic Opportunity

Digital skills help people apply for jobs, learn online, and even start small businesses in their communities.

High Demand

Millions need tech help. Few free, accessible resources exist. OpenByte fills a critical and underserved gap.
